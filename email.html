Email Marketing

Email marketing is a powerful way to directly connect with customers. For luxury watches, brands send personalized emails about offers, new collections, and reminders so users feel connected and confident before buying.

⚙ Backend Workflow (How Email Marketing Actually Works)

1. Collecting Emails (Database)
When users visit a website, sign up, or show interest through social media ads, their email addresses are stored in a database. This helps businesses connect with interested users. 👉 Example: A user visits a watch page and enters their email → the system saves it for future communication.
2. Email Service Provider (ESP)
Companies cannot send bulk emails directly through Gmail, so they use tools like Mailchimp or Brevo. These platforms help design emails, manage contacts, and send emails professionally. 👉 Example: Sending personalized emails to 1000 users at the same time.
3. Automation & Trigger System
The system tracks user behavior like clicks, views, or cart activity. Based on this behavior, it automatically decides which email to send (welcome, reminder, or offer). 👉 Example: User views a watch → system sends a reminder email after some time.
4. SMTP Server (Sending Process)
When you click "Send", the system uses SMTP (Simple Mail Transfer Protocol) to deliver the email through the internet to the user’s email provider like Gmail or Outlook. 👉 This is the actual backend process of sending emails.
5. Delivery & Spam Filtering
The receiver’s email service checks whether the email is safe or spam. If everything is valid, it appears in the Inbox or Promotions tab. 👉 Example: Good design + trusted sender = Inbox delivery ✅
